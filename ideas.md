# Design Brainstorm - The Anger Practice

## SELECTED: Idea A - Literary Journal × Editorial Magazine (probability 0.04)

**Design Movement:** Post-minimal editorial (Harper's × The Baffler × a 1970s rage-rock liner note)
**Core Principles:**
- Text-first. The reading is the design.
- Asymmetry over grids. Off-kilter mastheads, irregular gutters.
- Warmth without saccharine. Warm white + ember accent + ink black.
- Quiet typographic tension. Display serif against slender body serif.

**Color Philosophy:** Anger without aggression. Warm white (#FFFBF5) as page, deep charcoal (#2A2420) as ink, burnt orange (#C75C2A) as ember punctuation, deep crimson (#8B2500) for reserved emphasis. No neon. No purple. No gradient soup.

**Layout Paradigm:**
- Home: oversized editorial masthead, single vertical stream of issues (title + date + dek), no thumbnails, no cards, generous vertical air.
- Article: true two-column body (on desktop) with a wide intro column break, drop cap, floating share rail, full-bleed hero image above the headline, bio at bottom.

**Signature Elements:**
1. A burning-ember bullet ornament between sections (tiny filled ring in burnt orange).
2. Hand-drawn underline on accent words (SVG, rough-edged).
3. A thin charcoal rule that extends off-bleed to hint at the page edge.

**Interaction Philosophy:** Reader-first. Hover surfaces a subtle underline, no card lift, no purple shadow. Progress bar along the top of the article is a thin ember line.

**Animation:** Restrained. Subtle fade + 8px rise on section entrance. No parallax. No scroll-jacking. Images don't pan.

**Typography System:**
- Display: Playfair Display (700) for masthead and article H1.
- Body: Lora (400/500/italic) at 19px, line-height 1.85.
- Caption/meta: Inter (500) uppercased tracking for datelines only.
- Drop cap: oversized Playfair, ember color.

## Idea B (rejected) - Terminal / Zine / Riot (probability 0.02)
Black paper, mono type, red rubber-stamp ornaments. Too aggressive. The scope explicitly says "anger without aggression."

## Idea C (rejected) - Somatic Watercolor (probability 0.03)
Washy pastel backgrounds, soft watercolor hero art. Too placid for the topic. Would read as wellness-blog template.
